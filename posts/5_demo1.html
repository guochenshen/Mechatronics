System Demo 1 2/17/2016

Our team produced a demo of our system, showing a proof of concept for several aspects of the project. The first aspect was creating a physical prototype that was capable of moving around on its own. It was constructed of laser cut acrylic, which mounted two motors and the relevant electronics. The device responded to a limit switch on the front, causing the device to turn.

The second aspect was reading data from an IMU, which we use to read the angular position of the device. This, coupled with a few IR sensors, allows us to determine the absolute position in the 2D space, using the angle and the distance measurements.

The final aspect shown are stepper motors that respond to limit switches, representing the barrier crossing aspect of the device. These motors are representative of the drive system that will raise the device and allow it to cross the barrier.
